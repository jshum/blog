outline of article
researchers see a consistent millisecond timespan between high activity of trading operations
it's due to algorithm trading (my term for it)

execution algos - avoid losing money
execution algorithms which takes larger orders, break into smaller slices, choose the size of those slices and the times at which they send them to the market in such a way as to minimise slippage.
slippage is difference in price which happens when 'prices rise as you try to buy and fall as you try to sell'

types of execution algos:
volume participation algo - calculate the number of a company's shares bought and sold in given period, sends in a slice of the institution's overall order whose size is proportional to the calculated number, rationale being there will be less slippage when markets are busy

VWAP, volume weighted average price algo (most common) - using stats on volumes of shares that have traded in equivalent time periods on pervious days. 

another major class of algos - make money
electronic market making algos - continuously post a price that they will sell some shares at and a lower price which they will buy them, hoping to earn the spread
their reaction, response is much quicker - which leads to flood of orders and cancellations in short period of time

statistical arbitrage algos: search for transient disturbances in price patterns from which to profit.
price of corporation often fluctuates around relatively slow moving average. big order causes increase, big sell leads to temp fall. 
simple statistical arbitrate algos buy if stock is certain amount above, sell if stock is certain amount below.
more complicated ones involve multiple companies, algo dives in when the equilibrium breaks down and uses the fact that the stock reasserts itself to make money.

far more controversial algos are algo-sniffing algos.
algo-sniffing algo buy shares faster than VWAP, then sells them to VWAP at profit

spoofing - buy block of shares, issue LARGE number of buy orders just fractions below market price (so they don't actually buy it)
traders see lots of buy requests so they might buy it themselves.
then spoofer cancels buy requests and sells its holdings.
