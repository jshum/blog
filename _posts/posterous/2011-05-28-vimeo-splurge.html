--- layout: post title: VIMEO splurge published: true tags: [posterous] --- just spent two hours on vimeo, clicking every single mv link i could find. and here are the ones i like.
i realize everytime i come to hk, i splurge. not money but time on the internet. just hours and hours on end.
productive? not really. 
the issue which is at play here is how do you find good material on the internet without
a) spending time to look for it
b) spending time to use/hear/play it before you decide whether you like it.

i mean vimeo is a fucking awesome site. everything here is top quality. but even that, i still have to manually click through all the links and hear for myself before i can judge, right?
there are two assumptions i make when i look at this list.
a) i only looked through the vimeo list once. i'm ignoring all the ones i have ignored and will never see.
b) i only listened to them once. i ignored the ones that will grow on me after a couple listens.

so here is the list. the ones that i didn't miss and also the ones that i listened within 30 seconds of listening to it. now that i have time, i'll add some thoughts to the music as well.
the one thing i hate about my like of music over other art forms is that to fully appreciate it, u have to listen to the whole thing.
you can't make a catalog of music. unless u know each piece of music well enough, it's impossible to judge it just by looking at a bit of it.

apache - danger beach
i would categorize it as instrumental indie. overall: mild soothing song
i like the choice of timbres, overlay of acoustic guitars, guitar effects

hooray for earth - true loves
perfect song for slow hyping up moments, like fix you. wake up song i guess
interesting choice of timbres, percussive zings, solid hiphop beat, harmonized vocals
beats slowly get stronger, pump you up

alexander - a million years
happy song, single man walking across land song and achieving some epiphany
guitar + vocals with some harmony

bachna ae haseeno - bolly brook
interesting mix of indian music, with hiphop beat

manchester orchestra - simple me
in general, a beautiful song
sublime instrumental with inspiring vocals

kenton slash demon - daemon
my favorite of this bunch
mv is fucking awesome. using strings and nails to generate computer like effects
the song is pretty cool as well, auto tuned songs plus wicked beat using string like pizzicato

woodkid - iron
mv is freaking epic
organ, taikoo drum beat, brassy orchestral accompaniment,
the song is good for slow mo replays, contrast between good and bad factions, if song lyrics go well with story, can almost use as narration

flashman - the proposition
freestyle jazz/techno
amalgam of every possible sound.

metronomy - the look
electrokeyboard, drum, indie style production
goofy sad song for a comedy. guy gets dumped by girl song.

tv on the radio - will do
i have always disliked tv on the radio after that show for no reason.
their shit is pretty good in a serene setting but definitely not what i was hoping for spring concert
his vocals are pretty inspiring. hip hop beats, story telling from voice.

stanton warriors - turn me up some
mv is dancing battle between ballet, disco, hiphop.
song is  between hiphop beat, guitar solo
pretty repetitive.
