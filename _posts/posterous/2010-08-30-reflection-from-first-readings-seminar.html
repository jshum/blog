life is an oil painting. each person is a spotch of paint and everybody overlaps everyone else, symbolizing the effect we have on each other
at what point, what perspective should we introduce this world to the next generation? do we keep them objective, biased

Forgiving is considered a miracle in Catholicism
relates to how Anne didn't know how to explain why she loved him, because it was a miracle in itself. no reason, no explanation

what is the definition of forgiving? the different perspectives of forgiving in religion

is atonement quantitative?
the imbalance of justice. what is the atonement for such a crime? he couldn't die enough times to atone for his sins.
it's the interaction between forgiveness and guilt, both sides need to work for true forgiveness to be achieved. 
the forgiveness of victims, the forgiveness of the sinful themselves.
Ka's father is paranoid about being killed but how can he not be? how do we know the effect on the victim?

what can Ka do? forgive or carry it
shown in the different types of interaction with the past:
Nadine living with a burden in her
Claude accepting it
Danny living his whole life trying to avenge his father

the voicelessness of Nadine.
the parallel between Nadine and her patient, knowing who you are but not being able to tell it

my forgiveness is only towards fate. it's much more difficult to forgive a person or to live the guilt of having harmed someone. 
fate is so much easier to blame, to accept, to bow down to.
